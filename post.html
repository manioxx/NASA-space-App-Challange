Man Must Explore: The Urgency of Climate Exploration

Problems look mighty small from 150 miles up. Never in all their history have people been able truly to conceive of the world as one: a single sphere, a globe, where all directions eventually meet. There is no center, because every point, or none, is the center. It’s an equal Earth, which all people occupy as equals. As our atmosphere warms and our seas rise, humanity must realize that the Earth we share is fragile and finite.

Science cuts two ways, of course; it can be used for both good and ill. But there's no turning back from science. The early warnings about climate change, too, came from science. Now, it's science that provides the solutions—clean energy, sustainable farming, and carbon reduction.

What’s most significant about our current exploration of the climate is not only that we witness the changes, but that we can change our behavior. The journey toward a sustainable planet is our greatest challenge and opportunity.

A Chinese tale tells of some men sent to harm a young girl, but upon seeing her beauty, they become her protectors rather than her violators. That's how I felt seeing the Earth from space for the first time. I could not help but love and cherish her.

For those who have seen the Earth from space, and for the countless more who will in the future, the experience will certainly change your perspective. The things that we share in our world—air, water, soil—are far more valuable than those which divide us.

The Final Frontier: Protecting Earth for the Future

There can be no thought of finishing for 'aiming for the stars.' Both figuratively and literally, protecting the Earth and solving climate change is a task to occupy the generations. And no matter how much progress one makes, there is always the thrill of just beginning.

The dreams of yesterday are the hopes of today and the reality of tomorrow. Climate science has not yet mastered prophecy. We predict too much for the next year and yet far too little for the next decade. But one thing is certain: the work to protect Earth cannot be stopped. It is not the work of any one person or even a group of people. It is a historical process that humanity is carrying out in accordance with the natural laws of human development.

Reaching for the Stars: Humanity’s Journey in Space and on Earth

As we got further and further away from the Earth, it diminished in size. Finally, it shrank to the size of a marble—the most beautiful thing you can imagine. That beautiful, warm, living object looked so fragile, so delicate, that if you touched it with a finger it would crumble and fall apart. Seeing the Earth from space has to change a person.

To go places and do things that have never been done before—that’s what living is all about. As humanity reaches for the stars, it must also take care of the only planet that sustains life. Just as space is the final frontier, so too is climate change the ultimate test of our ingenuity and collective willpower.

Space, the final frontier. These are the voyages of a united humanity. Its mission: to explore sustainable futures, to seek out new technologies and cleaner energy, to boldly go where no generation has gone before.

As I stand out here in the wonders of the unknown, looking at our climate’s challenges, I realize there’s a fundamental truth to our nature: Man must explore, and this is exploration at its greatest.
