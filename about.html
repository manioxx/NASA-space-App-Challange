What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature and weather patterns. While natural factors like volcanic eruptions and solar cycles have influenced these shifts in the past, today's climate change is primarily driven by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels. This releases carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, trapping heat and causing global temperatures to rise.

Why Does It Matter?

The effects of climate change are being felt globally. Rising sea levels, more frequent and severe wildfires, extreme weather events like hurricanes and droughts, and the extinction of species are just a few of the impacts. It is not just about the environment—climate change also affects human health, food security, and access to clean water.

NASA’s Role in Climate Change Research

NASA plays a crucial role in understanding climate change through its extensive Earth observation programs. By using satellites, airborne missions, and ground-based research, NASA monitors key climate indicators such as rising global temperatures, shifting weather patterns, shrinking ice sheets, and increasing carbon dioxide levels. These data-driven insights help scientists and policymakers better understand the causes and effects of climate change. NASA's research also supports the development of innovative technologies aimed at mitigating climate impacts and ensuring the long-term sustainability of life on Earth.
